Alejandra Armijo is a Latina film editor originally from La Paz, Bolivia. She earned her MFA in Film Editing from the American Film Institute in 2023, following a 2020 internship with the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences that first pulled her into the industry.

Her AFI thesis work includes "Bajo La Tierra" and "The Bullfighter." Her short "Skin" won the Gold Medal at the 2023 Student Academy Awards and screened at Oslo Fusion International Film Festival.

Since then, she's built a freelance editing practice spanning feature films, shorts, and digital narrative content — cutting across drama, comedy, and horror, and carrying stories from assembly to final delivery. Selected work includes Hooperella and Rata (both through the LFI x Netflix Inclusion Fellowship), Tether, and Los Lamentos, alongside lead editor roles on serialized digital productions for MiniDramaStream (Dramabox) and Crazy Maple Studio (Reelshort).

Alejandra is a Gloria Schoemann Editors Fellow (The Latinx House x Adobe, 2024) and an alum of the Academy Gold Rising Program. She's currently expanding into animation editing, and remains endlessly curious about the psychology behind why stories move us — for her, editing is where a film finds its emotional truth, the place where an audience's catharsis actually gets built, frame by frame.
